About Me

SJ

I am a researcher at the University of Chicago, working at the intersection of observational cosmology and large-scale structure. My work focuses on understanding how matter is distributed across the universe and what this tells us about the fundamental physics governing cosmic evolution.

Using data from wide-field galaxy surveys and advanced computational techniques, I study the cosmic web — the vast network of filaments, walls, and voids that comprises the large-scale structure of the universe. I am especially interested in PSF modeling, galaxy morphology, and the statistical characterization of the matter distribution.

Research

Key areas of my ongoing and past work

🌌

Large-Scale Structure

Mapping the cosmic web through galaxy surveys, studying filaments, voids, and clusters to constrain cosmological parameters and understand structure formation.

🔭

Galaxy Morphology & Photometry

Developing advanced PSF subtraction and image processing pipelines for precise galaxy photometry, enabling cleaner measurements of galaxy structural properties.

💫

Cosmological Simulations

Running and analyzing N-body and hydrodynamic simulations (MDPL2, Uchuu, FLAMINGO) to model the dark matter distribution and compare with observational data.

📊

Statistical Methods

Applying Bayesian inference, MCMC sampling, Kernel Density Estimation, and Fourier analysis techniques to extract cosmological information from noisy datasets.
